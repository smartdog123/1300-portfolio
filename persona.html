Microwave Interface

This project is a close analysis done on microwave interface, specifically in regards to its design, interface and challenges to its usability. I observe real users interacting with an interface, interview these individuals about their experiences, create personas based on these users, and illustrate a storyboard for one of the personas.

Description

Problem to solve: Help users heat up food products.

The interface of microwave is showing on the right of the machine. There are four parts. The topmost one is a screen displaying the current state. The second one is used for different cooking mode (popcorn, beverage, etc.). The third one is used to set cooking time. And the bottom one is used for features of the microwave (cook time, steam clean, etc.). There are buttons for microwave settings. To open the microwave door, the user uses the handle on the door.

Observation

First Interviewee: Barry

Background: a college student with busy coursework

Observation: He opened the refrigerator and grabbed out the frozen food. He opened the microwave door and placed food in the center of microwave dish, and closed the door. He pressed “5” on the “express cook” shortcut button, and went to work on his homework. After five minutes, he checked the food, and he thought it was not cooked enough, so he added 2 minutes cooking time.

Second Interviewee: James

Background: a master’s student with part-time job

Observation: He grabbed a bag of microwave popcorn and placed food in the center of microwave dish. He pressed “Popcorn” on the cooking mode part, and the microwave just started without asking for cook time. Since he didn’t know how to set the cooking time for the Popcorn mode, he had to stand there waiting until he thinks the popcorn is ready. When there were little “pop” sound in the microwave, he believed it was fully cooked and grabbed the popcorn out.

Third Interviewee: Melinda

Background: an office lady with a family


Observation: She grabbed some leftovers from the refrigerator, and placed food in the center of microwave dish to reheat. She pressed the “cook time” button and set the timer to 5:30, and pressed the “start” button. Then she worked on making a salad for her children while the microwave was working. After she heard the “beep” sound from the microwave, she opened it, and the food was just well heated.

Interview Questions

  • Why do you use microwave instead of other cooking methods?

    • The three interviewees all said because the microwave is a fast and convenient cooking method. It doesn’t require you preheat. And you can just grab the food, put it in a bowl or on a plate, place it on the microwave dish, and it is ready to be heated. It is fast compared to an oven or a steamer, which requires preheat and should be careful while using. Although the interviewees are a little overwhelmed by lots of buttons on the interface, they could accept that.

  • How do you feel when you see the interface the first time?

    • The three interviewees all said they are a little overwhelmed by so much buttons on the interface, but they got more comfortable after taking a look into each one, since the name of each button indicate what they are used for. They expect the microwave to be an all-purpose one.

  • What microwave settings do you use (e.g. Popcorn, Beverage, normal, etc.) ?

    • Both Barry and Melinda use the normal cooking mode the most. Barry loves to use the “express cook” shortcut button on the interface, since it just require one press to set the cooking time and start cooking. Melinda usually set the cooking time manually, since she thinks this give her more precise control of how long the food is cooked, and she is experienced with the cooking time. James uses the “Popcorn” and “Potato” cooking mode the most since he mainly prepare popcorn and French fries using microwave.

  • Do you know how to use different features of the microwave?

    • The three interviewees all said they are not quite sure of how to use some features. Here are those features and the reason why they didn’t use them:
    Defrost: it requires user to input the weight of the food, but they don’t have kitchen scale.
    Steam Clean: they don’t know how it works, and there are no instructions, and they are afraid that the steam might short-circuit some electronics component.
    Power Level: when it is pressed, the screen just shows “cannot set power level at this time”, but doesn’t say when can the user set that.
    Reset filter: they don’t know how it works.
    Set Clock: they don’t know how it works.

  • Would you think of any difficulties when using the microwave interface?

    • James never figured out how to set the cook time for special cooking modes(Popcorn, Beverage, etc.), and he had to wait in front of the microwave till he thinks the food is ready, this is inconvenient for him. Barry and Melinda mainly stick to some specific settings, and they think it is hard to try other features since there are not enough instructions.

Persona

Description of the user: This is a persona describing a typical student at University with a lot of coursework or taking a part-time job. He would like to microwave some frozen food since he does not have enough time to cook for himself during the week. On his spare time, he love to have popcorn or French fries which can be quickly cooked as well.

Interface problem: He doesn’t have much experience on how long different food should be cooked, so he has to set an estimated time, then come and check whether the food is fully cooked. In addition, when he want to have popcorn or French fries, he has to wait in front of the microwave till he thinks they are ready.

Reasons behind the persona: The persona represent the thinking process when a person in a busy time and want a fast way to eat. And it also represent people who want to cook special foods like popcorn, potato, etc. So it is suitable for describing a student using microwave.

Description of the user: This is a persona describing a typical woman with work and a family. She would like to use microwave to reheat some leftovers. She wants more precise control of the cook time.

Interface problem: Since she wants more precise control of the cook time, she has to make several presses to start cooking. In addition, she doesn’t use internet to search pretty much, so those buttons without instructions are hard for her.

Reasons behind the persona: The persona represents how people with family would use the microwave. Since leftovers are common in families, reheating them is a common use case. Also, because people have a busy time working and taking care of their families, they don’t use internet very much, thus learning how to use some buttons become time-consuming for them. Therefore, this persona is representative enough.
